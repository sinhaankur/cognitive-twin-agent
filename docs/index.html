A private AI twin that runs entirely on your machine.

Vera reasons with a local LLM, keeps its own private memory of how you live, and — if you want — speaks in a loved one's cloned voice. Nothing syncs to a cloud. Nothing leaves your device unless you allow it.

🧠

Local LLM

Reasoning runs on a model on your machine (Ollama, LM Studio, Apple Intelligence). No prompts sent to a cloud by default.

🗂️

Private memory

What it learns about you is a local file, owner-only, on your device — never uploaded.

☁️

Zero cloud sync

No account, no server, no telemetry. There is nothing to sync and nothing to leak.

🔊

On-device voice

A loved one's voice, cloned locally (Coqui XTTS). The recording never leaves your Mac.

🔍

See how it thinks

A built-in "Brain" view shows its faculties and how a reply forms — from your real local data.

🧩

Picks the right brain

Policy-driven routing sends each request to the best local model for the job — inspectable, never cloud.

Twin Council new

One question, every voice. Ask all your twins at once — your mom, your dad, a mentor — and see each take side by side. Like the voices in your head, made explicit.

# ask everyone the same question
$ python -m cognitive_twin council "should I take the job offer?"

council › "should I take the job offer?"

  Anita »   (qwen2.5:7b)
    Take it — but negotiate the remote days first.
    You'll regret not asking.

  Dad »   (llama3.2)
    Money isn't everything. What's the commute
    do to your evenings?

  — 2 voices weighed in. The choice is yours.

Each twin answers as themselves, from its own persona and its own private memory. It never changes which twin is active, adds no dependencies, and runs entirely on your machine. In the interactive chat, /council <question> does the same without leaving the session.

What it can do

Not just answer when asked — it reaches out first, remembers you, and can act (permissioned).

  • Be you — a persona (likes, dislikes, values, style) you create, so it reasons and speaks as you.
  • Remember you — private, on-device memory of how you actually behave, folded into every answer. Clearable anytime.
  • Many twins — keep your mom, your dad, a mentor; each with its own persona, voice, and memory. Convene them as a council.
  • Talk — a native macOS Siri-style app: speak to it, it speaks back — optionally in a loved one's cloned voice.
  • Reflect — keeps thinking in the background about your projects; the next chat opens with a fresh thought it had while you were away.
  • See your screen & act — read what's on screen, open apps/URLs/Shortcuts — all opt-in, confirmed per action, reversible.
  • Research the web — search + read pages when you allow it (opt-in).

Quick start

A local model and a couple of commands. No account, no keys.

# 1. install Ollama (https://ollama.com) and pull a tool-capable model
$ ollama pull qwen2.5:7b        # or llama3.2, etc.

# 2. make it yours — guided first-run setup (name, persona, voice)
$ python -m cognitive_twin setup

# 3. talk to it
$ python -m cognitive_twin "good morning"
$ python -m cognitive_twin council "what should I do this weekend?"
$ python -m cognitive_twin voice --web    # 🎙 Siri-style voice UI

Want the native Mac app? cd macos/Vera && ./build-app.sh && open "Vera.app". Full docs, architecture, and the honest "what works today" table live in the README.
